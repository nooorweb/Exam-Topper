Conditional Sentences

English Grammar · 4 min read
Must Know
Overview

Conditional sentences describe hypothetical or real scenarios depending on specific conditions. Federal and provincial recruitment papers (FPSC, KPPSC, and ETEA) regularly test this topic to evaluate candidates' command of verb tense coordination and hypothetical structures.

Key Points

Zero Conditional

Refers to general truths, laws of nature, and scientific facts. Grammatical structure: If + simple present, simple present. (e.g. If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.)

First Conditional

Expresses real and highly possible future situations. Grammatical structure: If + simple present, will + infinitive verb form. (e.g. If he works hard for the exam, he will pass.)

Second Conditional

Describes imaginary, hypothetical, or highly unlikely present or future situations. Grammatical structure: If + simple past, would + base verb form. (e.g. If I won the lottery, I would buy a house.)

Third Conditional

Refers to imaginary past scenarios and unfulfilled events. Grammatical structure: If + past perfect, would have + past participle. (e.g. If they had played well yesterday, they would have won.)
